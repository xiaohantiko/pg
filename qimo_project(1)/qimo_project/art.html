Tea art is a sub-concept under the general concept of tea ceremony

It refers to the general term for all operations centered on tea in tea activities.

The tea art can be summarized as "the performance method of the tea ceremony".

Court Tea Ceremony

Court tea ceremony is a tea ceremony held by ancient emperors for worshiping gods, offering sacrifices, daily life or giving banquets to ministers. Tang Dynasty The Qingming tea feast, the Song Dynasty emperor Shi Xue bestowed tea, the Qing Dynasty thousand old man tea banquet and Qianlong's own Sanqing tea tea ceremony, Olive tea tea art and so on belong to court tea art. The palace tea ceremony is characterized by grand scenes, complicated etiquette, and solemn atmosphere. Strict, luxurious tea sets, strict hierarchy, and often have the color of political education and political orientation. since ancient times What is good at the top will be worse at the bottom. Historically, court tea art played a major role in promoting the development of tea art in my country effect.

Scholar Tea Art

Scholars are the main disseminators of tea culture in my country. "Since ancient times, celebrities have been romantic." Scholars regard "qin, chess, calligraphy, painting, poetry, wine and tea" as a symbol of literati's romanticism. Among them, tea understands the six arts and is very popular. Folk tea ceremony

Our country is a big family of 56 ethnic groups who depend on each other and coexist. Although each ethnic group has a common interest in tea, they have different customs of drinking tea. Han people mainly drink clear drinks, while ethnic minorities prefer mixed drinks. Inner Mongolian milk tea, Tibetan buttered tea, Uyghur fragrant tea, Hui three-paotai, Tu boiled tea, Dai bamboo tube tea, Bai three-course tea, Naxi dragon-tiger fight...the tea drinking methods of various regions and ethnic groups are colorful and diverse. These tea styles and customs are sublimated into tea art, which can not only greatly enrich the material and spiritual life of the people, but also form a positive interaction with the development of tourism. At the same time, folk tea art is often combined with folk music, folk costumes, folk songs and dances, and local specialty snacks, which are deeply loved by the masses.

religious tea ceremony

The Chinese government advocates freedom of religious belief, and religious tea art has positive significance for building a harmonious society. As early as a thousand years ago, Wang Shenzhi, King of Fujian, asked the national teacher, Monk Koubing, how to govern the country. Monk Koubing said: "Purify the heart with tea, and the country will be clean when the heart is clear. Use Zen to calm the mind, and the mind will be safe for all living beings." When all living beings are safe, the society will naturally be harmonious. The current common religious tea arts include Zen tea, Lifo tea, Guanyin tea, Tai Chi tea, Taoist health tea, etc. Religious tea art is characterized by special attention to etiquette, solemn and solemn atmosphere, simple and elegant tea sets, emphasizing self-cultivation or showing the way with tea.
